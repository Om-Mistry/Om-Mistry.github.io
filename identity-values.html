My identity and Values!

1. Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made and how you reflect on the decision now.

  • I can remember one ethical decision where I was driving, and I had the choice to let someone go by in-front of me. I weighed up the values involved, and I thought to myself about it. Is it safe to let them in? Yes, it was. Would I be late or frustrated if I let them in? No, I wouldn’t be, would someone else around me be frustrated or late? No there was no one behind me. So, I just let them in, simple, safe and everyone was happy. Looking back at my choice now I think about it and I’m very happy with how it went down.

2. Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

  • Something very important for me and my culture. Is how very important family is. Family is among the most important things for me. I want to be able to care completely for my grandparents, Mum, Dad, cousins, Aunts, Uncles and brother. They’re all extremely important for me and the thought of spending special bonding time with them is great. That’s why I’m so very family focused, this also links up with education and jobs. I want to have the best education and job opportunities possible so I can provide for them.

3. Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

  • My strengths I would say are being resilient, always trying my 100%, never giving up and trying to help others when I can. I’m resilient because no matter how much I’ve struggled or failed at something. I will always be getting up back off my feet and going again and again. Trying my best to learn and understand what I failed to do so in the past. This is why I’m also always trying my 100%, I always try my best to get something done and hate it when I must submit something that’s not done properly. Making sure everything is correct and done properly is a huge thing for me, I want to make sure everything I learn and do is proper. Also, when it comes to helping others. If I am experienced and knowledgeable in a subject, I will always try my best to find some time and help them. However I must be able to help myself understand it first, I would hate to be teaching someone something that’s incorrect.

4. Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

  • Some limitations are that I can get frustrated quite easily and that I can also be distracted easily. These can easily negatively impact my learning journey however I am in the process of changing myself and giving myself more chances to study in an isolated quiet area without distractions. This will allow me to reduce the risk of distractions quite easily. And when it comes to frustration, I will limit it by doing different activities, going outside, getting some exercise and taking small breaks before coming back and diving deep into what I did wrong and what I have to fix.
