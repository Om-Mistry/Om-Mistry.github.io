What is the difference between Margin, Border, and Padding?

    1. Margins:

  • Definition:
  • Margins are the space outside the border of an element, it's the distance between the border of the element and other elements surrounding it.
  • Purpose:
  • It helps control the spacing between things on pages, creating gaps between them
  • Example Code:
  • margin-top, margin-right, margin-bottom and margin left

    • 2. Borders:

  • Definition:
  • Border is the line that surrounds the element and it defines the boundary of an element.
  • Purpose:
  • It adds a visible boundary around an element, so it's seperated from surrounding spaces
  • Example Code:
  • border-width, border-style and border-color

    • 3. Padding

  • Definition:
  • Padding is the space between the content and the border of another element, it is the inner space of the element.
  • Purpose:
  • It controls the spacing between content and it's border, giving it space internally
  • Example Code:
  • padding-top, padding-right and padding-left
