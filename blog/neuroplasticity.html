Neuroplasticity

    Understanding neuroplasticity benefits people by:

  • Offering recovery from brain injuries and trauma
  • Encouraging lifelong learning to maintain brain health
  • Supporting mental well-being by showing that therapy can change bad thinking habits
  • It helps people learn and become better at handling tough situations

    • Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your benefit:

  • Try new things and learn new skills to keep your brain active and flexible
  • Spend a few minutes each day doing mindfulness exercises to keep your brain calm
  • Exercise regularly even if it’s something simple like walking or stretching, activity boosts brain health.
  • Eat well, sleep well and find ways to manage stress, healthy living helps your brain change and grow

    • What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity:

  • Learning a new skill, do activities that will challenge your brain. This will stimulate the brain and promote brain growth
  • Physical exercise such as walking, jogging and swimming has been shown to enhance neuroplasticity by increasing blood flow
  • Mindfulness and meditation will strengthen the brain, spending a few minutes each day can have long term benefits
  • Healthy diet will also be good for optimal brain function, eating a balanced nutrient rich diet supports the brain

    • Research growth mindset

  • Discuss what it is and why it’s relevant:
  • Growth mindset is the idea that intelligence, abilities and talents can be developed through dedication, effort and perseverance. People with a growth mindset embrace challenges and persist in the face of them. Also seeing failures as opportunities for learning and growth, this mindset increases resilience and the idea of taking risks

I realized I may have a growth mindset because I always try my best to persist through even the most complicated of things. Also I use my failures as a chance to learn from my mistakes and use them as a step towards improvement
  • How will I integrate growth mindset in my learning journey?:
